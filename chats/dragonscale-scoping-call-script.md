# DragonScale Build — Scoping Call Script
### Chris Takeuchi · 30 Minutes · Version 1.0

---

## BEFORE THE CALL

**Check:** Did they complete the assessment? Pull their report from Airtable before dialing.
**Goal:** By end of call, you know (1) their #1 pain, (2) the ROI number, (3) which tier fits, (4) whether they're a real buyer.
**Your mindset:** You are diagnosing, not pitching. If it's not a fit, say so. That integrity is the pitch.

---

## OPENING (Min 0–3)

> "Hey [Name], thanks for making the time. I've got 30 minutes blocked for us — I want to use it well for you, so I'll keep us moving. Quick agenda: I'll ask you some questions about what's going on in your business, we'll figure out where the biggest opportunity is, and I'll give you my honest take on whether a Build engagement makes sense and what that looks like. Sound good?"

**If they took the assessment:**
> "I pulled your report before this call — looks like [X] came up as your highest-friction area. I want to dig into that. Is that still top of mind or has something shifted?"

**If they didn't take the assessment:**
> "Before we dive in — the assessment takes 8 minutes and gives me a ton of context. Can I send you the link now and we reconnect in 15? Or we can work through some of those questions together right now."

---

## DISCOVERY (Min 3–18)

### Block 1 — Their World (5 min)

> "Tell me about your business — what do you do, how big is the team, and what does a normal week look like for you operationally?"

*Listen for:* Size, industry, whether they're owner-operated, how hands-on they are in ops.

> "What's the one thing in your operations that, if it ran itself, would change your week the most?"

*Listen for:* Specificity. Vague answers ("everything is manual") need drilling. Specific answers ("we spend 6 hours every Monday reconciling inventory across three systems") are gold.

---

### Block 2 — The Pain (5 min)

> "Walk me through that process right now, step by step. Who does it, how long does it take, what tools are involved, and where does it break down?"

*Listen for:* Human hours, frequency, error rate, downstream impact.

> "What happens when that process breaks or slows down? What's the cost — in time, money, stress, customer impact?"

*Listen for:* They need to feel the cost themselves. Don't rush past this.

> "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

*Listen for:* Past failures tell you what NOT to promise and where their skepticism lives.

---

### Block 3 — ROI Quantification (5 min)

Run the math out loud with them. Say:

> "Let me do some quick math with you, because I want to make sure we're talking about something that's actually worth your investment."

**Time saved:**
> "You said [Name/role] spends [X hours/week] on this. What's that person's fully loaded cost — salary plus benefits, roughly?"
> "[Hours] × [hourly rate] × 52 weeks = $[annual cost]. That's what this process costs you every year just in labor."

**Error cost (if relevant):**
> "You mentioned mistakes happen [X times/month]. What does one mistake cost you — returned orders, rework, customer calls?"

**Opportunity cost:**
> "What would [that person / you] do with those hours back? Is there revenue-generating work waiting?"

**The close on ROI:**
> "So conservatively, this process is costing you $[X] a year. A Build engagement in the Forge tier is $[price]. That's a payback period of [X months]. Does that math make sense to you?"

---

## RECOMMENDATION (Min 18–25)

Based on what you've heard, recommend a tier clearly and explain why:

**Spark:**
> "Based on what you've described, this is a single, well-defined process. I'd recommend our Spark tier — one automation, two weeks, $3,500 at founder rate. We go in, fix the one thing, and you get ROI fast."

**Forge:**
> "What you've described isn't one problem — it's three connected ones. If we only fix one, the other two will still slow you down. I'd recommend Forge: we map the full system, connect the automations, and you get a business function that actually runs. That's $8,500 at founder rate."

**Ascend:**
> "Honestly, what you're describing is a full function that needs to be rebuilt around AI. Your team is patching a process that was designed for a world before these tools existed. Ascend is the right move — we go deep, we rebuild it properly, and you never have to think about this again. $16,500 at founder rate."

**If it's not a fit:**
> "I'm going to be straight with you — based on what you've described, I don't think a Build engagement is the right move right now. Here's why: [reason]. What I'd actually recommend is [alternative]. I'd rather tell you that now than take your money and underdeliver."

---

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS (Use as needed)

**"That's more than I expected."**
> "I hear that. Can I ask — what were you expecting? [Listen.] Here's the thing: at your current cost of $[X/year] for this process, you're looking at a [X month] payback. Most of my clients find it's not really a cost question once we do the math — it's a timing question. What would need to be true for the timing to be right?"

**"We need to think about it."**
> "Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? [Listen.] Is it the ROI, the scope, the timing, or something else? I'd rather we work through whatever's in the way now than have you sit with an unanswered question."

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> "I can't move on price — the scope is what makes it fixed-price, and the price reflects real delivery. What I can do is scope down to Spark if you want to start smaller and build trust first. That's $3,500, two weeks, one automation. If it delivers, we go further. Fair?"

**"How do we know you can deliver?"**
> "That's the right question and I respect that you're asking it. I spent 10 years optimizing operations at Fortune 500 companies in CPG, pharma, and food & bev — I've seen versions of your problem at companies 100x your size. The difference is I'm not sending a junior consultant. I do the work myself, and I don't take on projects I'm not confident I can deliver. That said — the scoping process is designed to surface any risk before you sign. If I see something that concerns me, I'll tell you."

---

## CLOSE (Min 25–30)

**If it's a go:**
> "Great. Here's what happens next: I'll send you a fixed-scope proposal within 24 hours. It'll have exactly what we discussed — deliverables, timeline, payment terms. You review it, we sign, and I'll send you the kickoff questionnaire. We can be live in [X weeks]. Any questions before I put that together?"

**Payment framing:**
> "Terms are 50% upfront at signing, 50% at handoff. That protects both of us and keeps the engagement moving."

**If they need time:**
> "No problem. I'll send the proposal anyway so you have something concrete to review. Fair warning — I have [X] founder pricing spots left, and once they're gone the price moves to standard. I'll note the expiry in the proposal. Talk soon."
